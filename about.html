About Church


The Church Of God Kallumala


"The Church of God Kallumala has a rich and storied history rooted in the broader Pentecostal movement that swept through Kerala, India, in the early 20th century. This church, like many others in the region, was born out of a fervent desire for spiritual renewal and a deep commitment to the teachings of Pentecostalism, emphasizing the importance of the Holy Spirit's gifts and the personal experience of God's presence. Founded in Kallumala, a region known for its vibrant Christian community, the church initially served as a small congregation of believers who were passionate about spreading the Gospel and living out their faith in a tangible way. Over the years, The Church of God Kallumala grew in both size and influence, becoming a spiritual home for many in the community. The church's leadership, often drawn from local families with strong religious convictions, played a crucial role in shaping its doctrines and outreach programs. Throughout its history, the church has been involved in various social and charitable activities, reflecting its commitment to holistic ministry—addressing both spiritual and physical needs. The Church of God Kallumala has also been known for its dynamic worship services, vibrant youth programs, and a strong emphasis on Biblical teaching. As part of a larger network of Pentecostal churches in Kerala, The Church of God Kallumala has contributed to the spread of Pentecostalism in the region, influencing the faith journey of many believers. Today, it stands as a testament to the enduring power of faith and community, continuing to serve as a beacon of hope and spiritual guidance in the Kallumala area.

The Early Church (1973)
Ititthanam gives glory to God who has guided the church for the past 50 years. Looking back at the history of 50 years, there is not enough time to write and words to say... God did it and thank God. Born and raised in the Jacobite tradition, it was difficult for me to accept and follow the truths of the Pentecostal faith.In the 70s, divine visitations came to the house and Thankachan became ill. When medicine and mantras failed, prayer groups were sought for refuge. Through the prayers initiated by Nainachaya, God granted deliverances. On this occasion, Priya Nainachayan is remembered with gratitude. It was Nainachayan who tried to cooperate with Kallumala Kunjumonachayan. When everyone gave up, we were left alone with 3 children and had to leave home for the sake of faith. In this context, let us remember some of the servants of God who taught us the word that kept us firm in the faith in the early days. Can't forget Rani Kunjumon (Anjani) who taught the word of God, Chandapilla sir came with him and kept him fasting. When we started praying at home, we experienced a lot of adversity from our neighbors and relatives. God has maintained it until today. We gratefully remember all the servants of God who have ministered to Itti Thanam Church since its inception. Pastor James and Pastor Pappachan's services cannot be forgotten. Gratefully remember all those who ministered to God's church and families with faith even in a position where there was nothing financially. After the death of Kallumala Kunjumonchayan, some of the unspiritual ambitions for position in the churches are known. Praying that God will take over the leadership of the church going forward. So far the living God gives life and strength. There is weariness of age, loss of memory and weakness. All our 3 children are in USA and have been living with them for the past 20 years. Special thanks to all the believers of Ititthanam Church and God's servant. Although unable to attend our church's 50th anniversary, we remember everyone in our prayers. All will come, live your life according to God's word, may God help you to inherit eternity and remain faithful to the Lord until death. God bless the church. Wish you all the best. Pray for us too
